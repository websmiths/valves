RCA 6CU5

Entry #013 · Source: boxes-2.jpg

The 6CU5 is a 7-pin miniature beam power tube descended from the 50C5 / 50B5 family — the 6 V heater version sharing identical internal construction with the 12C5, 17C5, 25C5, and 50C5. It was used as an audio output valve in the output stage of television receivers and transformerless AC/DC radio sets.

Typical applications

The 6CU5 was primarily employed as the audio output tube in the sound section of television receivers and compact AC/DC radios of the late 1950s and 1960s. Its 7-pin B7G base allowed compact chassis layout, and its low operating voltage made it ideal for transformerless designs with line-derived B+ of around 100–130 V.
