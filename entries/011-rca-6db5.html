RCA 6DB5

Entry #011 · Source: boxes-2.jpg

The 6DB5 is a miniature noval beam power tube derived from the octal 6W6GT family — essentially a 6W6 with a reduced plate structure to fit a standard 9-pin envelope. Because of the smaller plate it cannot match the full plate dissipation of the 6W6GT, but it delivers useful audio output power from a compact package well-suited to post-war television and radio chassis.

Typical applications

The 6DB5 was designed primarily as a vertical deflection amplifier in television receivers, where its compact noval envelope was preferred over older octal types. It was also used as a single-ended audio output valve in compact receiver designs operating from low-voltage B+ supplies around 130–180 V.
