RCA 6AS8

Entry #014 · Source: boxes-2.jpg

The 6AS8 is a miniature noval single-diode / sharp-cutoff pentode combination tube used in AM/FM radio and television intermediate-frequency circuits. The diode section serves as a detector or AVC rectifier while the sharp-cutoff pentode provides IF amplification, combining two functions in one 9-pin envelope.

Typical applications

The 6AS8 was used in the intermediate-frequency strip of AM/FM radios and television sets, where its pentode section amplified the IF signal and its built-in diode provided signal detection and/or automatic volume control (AVC) rectification. Combining both functions in one envelope reduced component count and simplified circuit layout in compact receiver designs of the late 1950s.
