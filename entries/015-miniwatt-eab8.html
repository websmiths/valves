Miniwatt EAB8

Source: boxes-3.jpg

EAB8 is a 6.3 V–heated triple-diode valve on a 9-pin Noval (B9A) base, produced by Philips under the Miniwatt brand. Under the Mullard–Philips naming system, 'E' denotes a 6.3 V parallel heater, 'A' a single signal diode, 'B' a dual diode with common cathode, and '8' the Noval base series — making this a three-diode combination valve. It is a niche type rarely listed in general catalogues and should be physically verified; the printed code on the crop is small and pixelated.

Typical applications

The EAB8 triple-diode configuration allows a single valve to perform AM envelope detection, automatic gain control (AGC) rectification, and one branch of an FM ratio detector simultaneously. Such multi-diode noval valves were common in compact European AC-mains radio designs of the 1950s that needed to minimise valve count. The independent cathode on the 'A' diode section allows it to float at a different potential from the common-cathode 'B' pair, enabling simultaneous AM and FM detection duties.
