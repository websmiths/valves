RCA 5Z3

Entry #001 · Source: boxes-1.jpeg

Full-wave high-vacuum rectifier with a 4-pin UX base. Used in 1930s–40s valve receivers and amplifiers where a high-current B+ supply was needed. Electrically identical to the more familiar octal-based 5U4G, just on the older 4-pin socket.

Typical applications

HT power supply rectifier in valve radios (broadcast and communications receivers, 1933–50s), Hammond-style organs, early hi-fi and guitar amplifiers, and laboratory power supplies needing a high-current 5V-heated rectifier on the older 4-pin socket.
