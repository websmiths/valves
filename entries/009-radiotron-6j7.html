Super Radiotron 6J7

Entry #009 · Source: boxes-1.jpeg

Sharp-cutoff RF / IF / detector pentode on the octal base — the direct octal successor to the pre-octal 6C6. Top-cap control grid, 6.3 V indirectly-heated cathode, used as the first audio amp, plate detector or low-noise RF stage in 1937–50s receivers and signal generators.

Typical applications

Octal version of the 6C6 — fixed-gain RF pentode with low noise and a sharp cutoff, used as the first IF stage, as a plate-detector, or as a low-distortion audio voltage amplifier. Common in laboratory signal generators (e.g. BC-221 frequency meter) and in PA systems alongside the remote-cutoff 6K7.
