Dual triode (VHF / audio)
Valve Catalogue · Entry

Miniwatt 7DJ8

Source: boxes-3.jpg

7DJ8 (European designation PCC88) is a miniature 9-pin Noval dual triode with frame-grid construction, 7 V series heater, and exceptionally high transconductance of ~12,500 µmhos. Developed by Philips in the late 1950s as the series-heater counterpart of the ECC88 / 6DJ8, it was designed primarily for VHF cascode RF amplifier stages in television tuners. It has since become highly sought-after in high-end audio preamplifier applications.

Typical applications

The 7DJ8/PCC88 was designed specifically for VHF television tuner cascode amplifier stages, where its low noise and high transconductance (~12,500 µmhos) gave it an advantage over earlier dual triodes. Its 7 V heater suited European series-heater television receiver designs. In contemporary high-end audio, it is widely used as a direct plug-in alternative to the 6DJ8/ECC88 and 6922/E88CC family — the 1 V heater difference is tolerated in most circuits — with Matsushita (National/Panasonic) and Philips Miniwatt examples particularly prized for their sonic character and build consistency.
