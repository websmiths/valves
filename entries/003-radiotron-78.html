Radiotron 78

Entry #003 · Source: boxes-1.jpeg

Variable-mu (remote-cutoff) RF / IF pentode introduced by RCA in 1932. Effectively identical electricals to the better-known 6D6 — same heater, same 6-pin small base, same characteristics — and the two are routinely listed as direct equivalents.

Typical applications

Standard variable-mu pentode in mid-1930s superhet broadcast and short-wave receivers — the RF and IF stages of almost every domestic radio from about 1933 to 1936, before the octal-base 6U7G and miniature 6BA6 took over. Pairs naturally with the sharp-cutoff 6C6 as detector / first-AF.
